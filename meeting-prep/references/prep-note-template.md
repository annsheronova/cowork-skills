# Prep note template

Use this structure for the Notion page. Keep the whole page to roughly one screen — if you're scrolling, you've written too much.

## Section order

```
# [Meeting Title]
[date] · [start–end time] · [location / video link]

## At a glance
[2–4 lines. What this meeting is, who called it, what the user needs to walk in knowing. This is the BLUF. If they read nothing else, they should still be prepared.]

## Attendees
- **[Name]** — [Title, Company]. [One sentence on who they are / why they're in the room.]
  - *Last email:* [date] — [1-line summary of the thread]
  - *Context:* [LinkedIn-style one-liner if external; recent news if notable; "internal, reports to X" if internal]

(repeat per attendee; keep internal attendees shorter than external ones)

## Recent context
[Everything the user should remember going in — open threads, promises made, past decisions, relevant project state. Bullets, 3–6 max.]

## Likely agenda
[From calendar description if present; else inferred. Mark inferred items with "Unverified:"]

## Talking points (three)
1. [Sharp, specific. What the user should raise or be ready to answer.]
2. […]
3. […]

## Watch-outs
[Optional. Sensitivities, people who may push back, things NOT to raise. Leave out the section if there aren't any.]
```

## Length guidance

- At a glance: 2–4 lines, ever.
- Attendee blocks: 2–4 lines per person for externals, 1 line for internals.
- Recent context: 3–6 bullets.
- Talking points: exactly three. Not "three to five" — three. Forcing the choice is the value.
- Watch-outs: 0–3 bullets, only if real.

Total target: fits on one laptop screen without scrolling (~400 words).

## Writing examples

**Weak At-a-glance:**
> This is a meeting with Alex Chen to discuss the pilot. Alex works at Acme. The meeting is at 2pm.

**Strong At-a-glance:**
> Mid-pilot check-in with Alex Chen (Acme VP Product). Pilot was tracking green until March; last email Mar 14 flagged a 2-week slip on the data-import milestone. Alex wants to talk scope — be ready to offer a narrower v1 or a new date.

**Weak talking point:**
> 1. Discuss the timeline.

**Strong talking point:**
> 1. Offer two concrete options for the timeline: (a) ship narrower v1 on original date, (b) full scope four weeks later. Alex has said twice she prefers a firm date over a feature count.

## Markers for uncertainty

Use `Unverified:` as an inline prefix for any claim you couldn't confirm in the sources. One word. Example:

> *Unverified:* Alex was recently promoted (saw a mention in an article but not her LinkedIn).

This lets the user eyeball in two seconds what's solid vs. what they should double-check.
