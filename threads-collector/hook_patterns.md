## Threads-specific context (why these patterns, not Twitter's)

Before the catalog — three mechanics that shape what works on Threads in 2026 and why the patterns below are different from a Twitter/X hook list:

1. **Replies count as much as posts.** Adam Mosseri has publicly stated that the sum of a user's replies contributes to growth about as much as the sum of their posts. Buffer's 2026 data shows replying to comments on your own posts lifts engagement ~42% — the highest platform lift they've measured. **Implication**: hooks that invite threaded back-and-forth beat hooks that broadcast a take.
2. **Engagement velocity > total engagement.** A post that collects 20 replies in 30 minutes outranks one that collects 50 replies over 24 hours. **Implication**: fast-reply hooks (questions, contradictions, fill-in-the-blank) outperform slow-burn essay hooks.
3. **Two-line truncation is the real constraint, not the 500-character limit.** The feed truncates at ~100 characters. The first two lines do all the work — everything after "more" is a bonus, not the hook. **Implication**: numbered lists and open-loop formats that force the "more" tap perform disproportionately.

## Seeded patterns (15)

### 1. Specific-number flex
**Definition:** Open with an oddly specific number tied to a personal result.
**Why on Threads:** Odd numbers read as real (not rounded marketing). Invites the reply "how?" — which is what the algorithm rewards.
**Examples:**
- "2 years ago I jumped on Threads with 52 Followers. Today I have 58,000+ Thriends." — @heythemnaji
- "I gained 2,847 followers in 30 days using one strategy" (template-grade, miraflow.ai catalog)
**Template:** `[N specific] [time unit] ago I had [small odd number]. Today I have [big odd number].`

### 2. Contrarian flip
**Definition:** State a common belief, flip it, promise the why.
**Why on Threads:** Triggers the reply-debate loop. Explicitly named as "The Contrarian Hook" by Threads growth accounts.
**Examples:**
- "Most creators think they need more followers. They actually need clear positioning." — @digitalalliancehq
- "Forget TikTok. Forget Instagram. Threads is the easiest platform to grow in 2025." — @creatortadeaas
**Template:** `Most [group] think [X]. They actually need [Y].`

### 3. Confession-as-authority
**Definition:** Lead with a vulnerable admission that re-frames as credibility.
**Why on Threads:** Unpolished confession reads human, draws reply-style responses (not just likes). Caveat: Mosseri's January 2026 observation that authenticity is "infinitely reproducible" — formulaic versions of this are starting to burn out. Genuine specificity still works.
**Examples:**
- "Over the past year, I've gained thousands of followers and left my corporate job for good. Want to know a secret?" — @hopeengineer
- "WE DID IT!! I finally made my first 1K online!" (cited by @creatortadeaas)
**Template:** `[Embarrassing or humble starting state]. [Surprising current state]. [Open loop — 'want to know how?'].`

### 4. Before-Path-After
**Definition:** Three-beat identity shift compressed into the hook — past self, transition implied, present self.
**Why on Threads:** Scannable on mobile inside the 2-line truncation. Invites "same" / "how?" replies.
**Examples:**
- "Me at 17: Alcohol, Partying, Video games. Now me at 20: Gym, Studying, Building my business." (cited by @creatortadeaas)
**Template:** `Me at [past age]: [3 noun phrases]. Me now at [current age]: [3 noun phrases].`

### 5. List-title open loop
**Definition:** Announce a numbered list you haven't delivered yet — force the "more" tap.
**Why on Threads:** Truncation forces readers to expand. Delivery in reply-thread extends session time, which the algorithm ranks on.
**Examples:**
- "I've posted 500+ times on Threads. Here are 7 types of posts that always go viral (with real examples):" — @creatortadeaas
- "I used these 6 viral post templates (copy-paste to get the same results):" — @heythemnaji
**Template:** `I've [credential action with number]. Here are [N] [thing] that [outcome]:`

### 6. Industry-drama piggyback
**Definition:** Quote a real, fresh platform/industry stat as the entire hook.
**Why on Threads:** Threads rewards freshness sharply. News-reaction hooks ride the existing curiosity wave.
**Examples:**
- "Threads just overtook X!! It now has 130.2 million daily users. X has only 130.1 million."
- Matt Navarra's algorithm-news posts are the archetype.
**Template:** `[Platform] just [verb] [competitor]. [Specific stat]. [Specific stat].`

### 7. Pattern-interrupt imperative
**Definition:** Command to stop doing the obvious thing, then redirect.
**Why on Threads:** "Stop" verb survives the 2-line truncation as a literal attention-break. Reads as advice, not brag.
**Examples:**
- "Stop posting motivational quotes. Here is what actually grows your account." (miraflow.ai catalog)
- "MASTER THE HOOK. Your first 2 lines = life or death." — @creatortadeaas
**Template:** `Stop [common behavior]. Here's what actually [outcome].`

### 8. Secret-reveal
**Definition:** Claim a hidden mechanism, promise to share.
**Why on Threads:** Pairs with reply-heavy algorithm — "drop it" replies are high-quality signals.
**Examples:**
- "Want to know a secret? I use the same 3-part formula for every single hook:" — @hopeengineer
- "The one thing I changed in my bio that tripled my profile visits"
**Template:** `The one [small thing] I changed that [disproportionate outcome]:`

### 9. Question-as-post
**Definition:** A post that IS the question. No setup, no context.
**Why on Threads:** SociaVault 2026 data: question-ending posts 2–3x reply count. Pure-question posts maximize this. (See anti-patterns — there's a line between genuine question and engagement bait.)
**Examples:**
- "What's the career advice you wish you got at 22?" (template-grade, 2026 strategy guides)
**Template:** `[One sentence, ends with '?']. No other text.`

### 10. Hot-take declaration
**Definition:** Label the take explicitly as unpopular before stating it.
**Why on Threads:** The "unpopular opinion" label primes the reader to disagree — disagreement means reply, which the algorithm weights highest.
**Examples:**
- "Breaking News: I have a hot take that may be a unpopular opinion…" — @jhopp217
- "Most Threads advice is wrong. Here is what the data actually shows" (miraflow.ai)
**Template:** `Unpopular opinion: [specific claim most of your niche disagrees with].`

### 11. Fill-in-the-blank
**Definition:** Sentence with a literal underscore gap for readers to complete.
**Why on Threads:** Lowest-friction reply mechanism. Still working in 2026 — watch anti-patterns below, this is on the edge of engagement-bait enforcement.
**Examples:**
- "The hardest part of my job is _______."
- "____ is overrated. ____ is underrated."
**Template:** `[Setup sentence with one or two ____ gaps].`

### 12. Plain-language contradiction
**Definition:** Two short sentences where the second contradicts the first. No hedging words.
**Why on Threads:** Fits the 100–280 char sweet spot. Contradiction creates the open loop inside the visible truncation window.
**Examples:**
- "Ego says: 'Don't be too vulnerable.' Wisdom says: 'Authenticity builds real connection.'" — @wholistic.scott
- "Nobody talks about this: [X]. Everyone should."
**Template:** `[Short voice] says: '[common fear].' [Contrasting voice] says: '[reframe].'`

### 13. Single-image + one-liner
**Definition:** One strong image; one declarative line of text. No paragraph.
**Why on Threads:** Images reportedly outperform pure text by ~60%. Single-line captions avoid truncation entirely.
**Examples:**
- Tadeas's "SINGLE IMAGE" category; Shakira's rehearsal-clip posts (7.5M launch-week interactions).
**Template:** `[One declarative sentence].` + [visual]

### 14. Mosseri-quote / authority-citation
**Definition:** Open with a verbatim quote from a known platform authority.
**Why on Threads:** Pulls in the authority's credibility; quote-replying @mosseri is itself a documented growth tactic.
**Examples:**
- "Adam Mosseri: 'If you're really trying to grow, reply much more than you post.'"
- Matt Navarra's executive-quote + analysis format.
**Template:** `[Authority name]: '[short verbatim quote].' Here's what this means for [your niche]:`

### 15. Receipts-first
**Definition:** Lead with proof (stat, screenshot caption, credential) BEFORE any claim.
**Why on Threads:** The "3Ps" (Proof / Promise / Plan) formula cited across 2026 breakdowns — claim-first reads as marketing on Threads, proof-first reads as credible.
**Examples:**
- "$47K in 6 months from a Threads account I started on a train."
- "30M impressions in 24 hours — here's the post that did it:" (MrBeast launch pattern)
**Template:** `[Concrete metric with $ or #]. Here's [what I did / what it means]:`

## Anti-patterns (underperform specifically on Threads in 2026)

### A1. Generic engagement bait
**Pattern:** "Comment your age and I'll guess your job" / "This or that" prompts with no personal stake.
**Why it fails:** Mosseri explicitly named this as the target of 2024–2026 enforcement. Short-term replies, long-term account down-ranking. Trains the algorithm to classify the account as low-quality.

### A2. Twitter-style mega-threads (1/12, 2/12…)
**Pattern:** Numbered multi-post chains imported from X muscle-memory.
**Why it fails:** Threads' 100–280 character sweet spot and the truncation UX punish long chains. SociaVault 2026 data: single strong posts beat multi-part threads on engagement velocity. Only split if each part leads *naturally* into the next — that's a much higher bar than on X.

### A3. Naked link posts
**Pattern:** Post body is just a link or a link + one sentence.
**Why it fails:** Observed 30–50% lower engagement. Mosseri says there's no policy penalty, but links pull users off-platform, killing reply velocity — which IS what the algorithm ranks on. (Contradiction flagged: the official line and the observed data disagree. Safer read: links don't kill reach, they kill replies.)

### A4. Over-polished brand-voice broadcasts
**Pattern:** Corporate press-release cadence; everything is "we're excited to announce."
**Why it fails:** Near-zero reply velocity. Buffer's 2026 creator roundup calls out Wendy's and Tinder specifically because they ditched the corporate handbook. Monologue voice signals "don't reply," which is exactly the signal the algorithm punishes.

### A5. Motivational-quote hooks
**Pattern:** "You got this." "Believe in yourself." "Discipline > motivation."
**Why it fails:** No open loop, no debate, nothing to reply to. Reads as filler. Explicitly the thing the "Stop posting motivational quotes" pattern-interrupt is targeting.

### A6. Authenticity theater
**Pattern:** Formulaic vulnerable confession ("I cried today because…") with no specific stakes.
**Why it fails:** Per Mosseri's January 2026 "authenticity is infinitely reproducible" observation and the 2026 backlash documented across creator media, templated confession posts now read as performance. Genuine specificity (Pattern 3 above) still works — the *formula* version is what's burning out.
